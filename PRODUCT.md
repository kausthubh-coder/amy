# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Amy is for Android users who want calorie tracking to feel like writing a quick note instead of operating a database-heavy diet app. They are often logging while eating, after a meal, or from a home-screen widget, so the primary workflow needs to stay fast, forgiving, and editable.

## Product Purpose

Amy helps users log meals line by line, estimate calories and macros from optional AI/photo inputs, scan packaged foods with Open Food Facts, and review daily progress without an account. Success means a user can capture what they ate, see the right calorie/macro totals, correct mistakes, and trust the local data.

## Brand Personality

Fast, focused, personal. Amy should feel compact and direct, with enough personality from the cat icon and flame calorie language to feel approachable without becoming decorative or noisy.

## Anti-references

Do not turn Amy into a generic health dashboard, meal-plan marketplace, or spreadsheet-style nutrition tracker. Avoid water tracking, menu scanning, USDA/FatSecret dual lookup, account-first flows, large marketing surfaces, and UI that hides the user's actual logged food behind abstract streaks or charts.

## Design Principles

- Logging is the product: one line, one food or meal, one editable nutrition result.
- Show the user's real data before summaries: totals, consumed amounts, goals, and streak continuity must be explicit.
- Keep capture paths honest: photo, label, barcode, dictation, and saved-meal actions should produce visible log lines or clear errors.
- Preserve local-first trust: data stays on-device unless the user chooses Open Food Facts, location context, or OpenRouter.
- Use familiar mobile controls: compact tabs, clear icon buttons, stable rows, and predictable modals.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use readable contrast on the dark interface, keep touch targets large enough for one-handed Android use, support reduced motion where animation is decorative, and avoid communicating state through color alone.
